Q Branch is the three-man team responsible for Vesper, the simple and elegant tool for collecting notes.

How are you using Glassboard?

We use Glassboard for day-to-day communication between the three of us. John and I use iMessage for rapid design discussion, but for anything over the threshold of nerding out over font weights or nit-picking comps, we turn to Glassboard. And not just product discussion; we cover everything from business paperwork to travel arrangement through Glassboard. As far as we’re concerned, email doesn’t exist.

Externally, Glassboard is how we communicate with our beta team. A lot of the betas I participate in feel like one-sided conversations. We wanted to give our team a way to communicate not just with us, but with each other. We get a lot of great feedback and ideas that way. Brent has used email discussion lists in the past, but Glassboard seems to facilitate more interesting conversations.

Why Glassboard?

The easy answer is Brent, but that’s not the whole story. We’ve explored other options and have even tried a few out. Glassboard’s web app, iOS app, notifications, and logical hierarchy work for us in a way that nothing else seems to. Email fails because it’s too inclusive of the outside world. For a virtual company like ours, we need a safe space to call our own.

If not Glassboard what would you use?

I’m just guessing here, but probably something like IRC for chat and email for heavier, asynchronous threads.
